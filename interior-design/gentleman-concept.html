The Entrance

Red Glow on the Sidewalk

The sign is visible from a block away — neon red tubes tracing "BLUES" and "BURGER" on a matte black board, mounted on brass brackets that catch the streetlight. Below it, a hand-forged Route 66 shield in neon blue marks the threshold. The glass double doors are framed in brushed brass. Through them, the warm red interior beckons. An American flag rendered entirely in neon tubes glows beside the entrance — not patriotic kitsch, but a gentleman's nod to the culture that made this food, this music, this feeling.

The Gentleman's Booth

Oxblood & Brass

These aren't diner booths — they're thrones. Deep-tufted oxblood leather with diamond-pattern stitching, brass-capped armrests, and walnut partition walls that rise high enough to give every party privacy. The tables are solid mahogany with brass edge inlay. Above each booth, a brass pendant light hangs from a fabric cord, casting a warm pool that makes the leather glow. Between booths, the exposed brick wall holds black-and-white photographs of B.B. King, Muddy Waters, and the Chicago blues scene — framed in matte black with brass nameplates. A single neon tube traces a horizontal line along the brick, casting the entire wall in deep red.

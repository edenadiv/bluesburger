Storefront

The Sign

Fremont Street Meets Ibn Gvirol

You see it from a block away. An ornate gold neon border — scrollwork and curves like a vintage Vegas marquee — frames the name in blazing red neon script. Below, "OLD NORTH TLV" pulses in cool blue. A neon guitar silhouette in green traces Lucille above the sign. The gold border throws warm halos onto the brick. At street level, a classic "OPEN" sign in pink neon sits in the window beside a Route 66 shield in blue. Every tube is hand-bent glass filled with noble gas — no LEDs, no shortcuts. This is the real thing, and everyone on the street knows it.

Seating

The Booths

Neon-Lit Leather

Each booth sits under its own neon accent — a thin gold tube tracing a gentle arc along the wall above, casting everything in warm amber. The seats are deep oxblood leather with diamond tufting and brass button centers. Between booths, a vertical red neon tube runs floor to ceiling, a gentle divider that makes each table feel like its own room. On the far wall, a large neon outline of a Cadillac tail fin in blue stretches eight feet wide — the ultimate American icon rendered in light. Framed photos of Memphis juke joints hang beside it in brass frames.

Gold Neon Arc Above Booths Red Neon Vertical Dividers Blue Neon Cadillac Tail Fin Diamond Tufted Oxblood Brass Button Centers Memphis Juke Joint Photos
The Bar

The Bourbon Bar

Gold & Red Glow

Behind the bar, a massive neon sign spells "BOURBON" in ornate gold tubes with red accents — the B and N wrapped in decorative scrollwork like a vintage liquor advertisement. The mirrored back wall doubles every bottle and every tube of neon, creating an infinity of warm light. Below the sign, two shelves hold forty bottles of whiskey, rye, and bourbon. The bar counter is hand-finished mahogany with a brass rail and foot rest. Above, a row of small green neon stars traces the ceiling line — a subtle Vegas touch that ties the whole room together.

Gold "BOURBON" Neon Ornate Tube Scrollwork Mirrored Back Bar Green Neon Star Row Mahogany Counter Brass Rail & Foot Rest
Music

The Corner Stage

Where the Blues Live

The stage is framed — literally. An ornate gold neon border, identical in style to the exterior sign, traces the proscenium arch above the performance area. Inside the arch, the word "LIVE" pulses in red neon. Below, a raised oak platform holds the amps, the mic, and the magic. A green neon guitar outline — Lucille herself — glows on the brick wall behind the drummer. The spotlight rig overhead mixes with neon to create a light environment that no Instagram filter can improve upon. This is why people come back every Thursday.
